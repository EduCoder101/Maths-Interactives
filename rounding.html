What is Rounding?

Rounding is a way to simplify numbers by replacing them with nearby 'benchmark' numbers that are easier to work with. We use rounding when we need a quick estimate rather than an exact answer.

Example: If you have $24.75 and want to know roughly how much that is, you could round to $25 ‚Äî it's close enough for quick thinking!

The Midpoint Rule

  • If a number is below the midpoint, round down to the lower benchmark
  • If a number is at or above the midpoint, round up to the higher benchmark
  • Example: Rounding 24 to tens ‚Üí midpoint is 25, so 24 rounds down to 20
  • Example: Rounding 57 to tens ‚Üí midpoint is 55, so 57 rounds up to 60
