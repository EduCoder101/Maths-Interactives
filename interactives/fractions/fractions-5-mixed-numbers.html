Fractions V

Mixed numbers: changing an improper fraction into a whole and a part

Learning Intention & Success Criteria ▼

Learning Intention

We are learning to change an improper fraction into a mixed number by dividing the top number by the bottom number.

Success Criteria

  • I can recognise an improper fraction and a mixed number
  • I can divide the numerator by the denominator to find the whole number
  • I can put the remainder over the denominator to make the fraction part
1
Mixed numbers

A mixed number has a whole number and a fraction together, like 134.

One whole bar and three quarters of another
2
Improper fractions

An improper fraction has a top number bigger than the bottom number, like 74. It is the same amount as the mixed number above.

Seven quarters shaded in total, which is the same as 1 and three quarters
3
Change it by dividing

To change an improper fraction into a mixed number, divide the top by the bottom. The answer is the whole number, and the remainder goes over the denominator.

7 ÷ 4 = 1 remainder 3

So 74 = 134

4
Worked example

Change five halves into a mixed number.

52

Divide the top by the bottom: 5 divided by 2. How many whole twos are in five, and what is left over?

5 divided by 2 = 2 remainder 1. So five halves equals 212.
✓
Your turn

Change eleven thirds into a mixed number. Work it out, then reveal.

113

11 divided by 3 = 3 remainder 2. So eleven thirds equals 323.
