Fractions III

Comparing fractions: which is greater, smaller or equal?

Learning Intention & Success Criteria ▼

Learning Intention

We are learning to compare two fractions using bars of the same length, and to choose the greater than, less than or equal to symbol.

Success Criteria

  • I can compare two fractions by lining up bars of the same length
  • I can explain that a bigger denominator means smaller pieces
  • I can choose the correct symbol to show which fraction is greater, smaller or equal
1
Use bars the same length

To compare two fractions fairly, draw them on bars that are the same length, then see which has more shaded.

12
14

A half fills more than a quarter, so a half is the bigger fraction.

2
Bigger bottom, smaller pieces

When the top numbers are the same, the fraction with the bigger denominator has the smaller pieces.

14
18

One eighth is a smaller piece than one quarter, so a quarter is greater.

3
Comparing with a whole

A fraction less than one whole does not fill the bar completely.

23
1

Two thirds does not fill the whole bar, so two thirds is less than one whole.

4
The symbols

We show the comparison with a symbol. The wide open end always faces the bigger amount. Equal amounts use the equals sign.

Compare one third and one sixth.

13
16
One third fills more than one sixth, so one third is greater. We write it with the open end of the symbol facing the third.
