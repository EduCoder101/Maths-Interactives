🤔 What is a decimal?

💬 Ask the class

What is a decimal? When you hear the word "decimal", what does it mean?

Definition

A decimal is a number that shows a part of a whole number. The digits after the decimal point represent parts that are smaller than one.

The word "decimal" comes from the Latin word decem, meaning ten, because our number system is based on groups of ten.

The ×10 and ÷10 Pattern

🔢 Place value above zero

Each place value column is 10 times larger than the one to its right, and 10 times smaller than the one to its left.

⭐ Key Insight

The ÷10 rule never stops. After tenths come hundredths, then thousandths, then ten-thousandths… The place value system extends infinitely in both directions.

⭐ The Rule

We aren't just adding new columns, we are applying the "divide by 10" rule recursively. Each place is always 1/10 the size of the place to its left.

Phase 3 · Explicit Instruction
The Zoom, looking inside the gaps

🔍 What's between 0 and 1?

On a number line, there's a big gap between 0 and 1. But that gap is full of numbers. We just need to zoom in to see them.

Each time we zoom in, we divide each section into 10 equal parts, the same ÷10 rule that governs the entire place value system.

Zoom Level 1

Whole numbers → Tenths

Start with a number line from 0 to 1. Divide it into 10 equal parts. Each part is one tenth (0.1).

Number Line: 0 to 1 (divided into tenths)
🔍 Zoom 1 Summary

The gap between 0 and 1 contains 10 equal parts. Each part is 0.1 (one tenth). Notice that 0.5 sits exactly in the middle, our number from the hook!

Zoom Level 2

Tenths → Hundredths

Now zoom into the gap between 0.1 and 0.2. Divide that gap into 10 equal parts. Each part is one hundredth (0.01).

Zooming into 0.1 to 0.2 (divided into hundredths)
🔍 Zoom 2 Summary

The gap between 0.1 and 0.2 contains 10 equal parts. Each part is 0.01 (one hundredth). So 0.15 is exactly halfway between 0.1 and 0.2.

Zoom Level 3

Hundredths → Thousandths

Zoom in again, into the gap between 0.12 and 0.13. Divide into 10 equal parts. Each part is one thousandth (0.001).

Zooming into 0.12 to 0.13 (divided into thousandths)
🔍 Zoom 3 Summary

The gap between 0.12 and 0.13 contains 10 equal parts. Each part is 0.001 (one thousandth). The same rule applies every time, divide by 10!

♾️ How far can we go?

💬 Discussion

If a student wants to write a hundred-thousandths column, or a millionths column, can they? Why or why not?

✅ Yes, infinitely!

The ÷10 rule never stops. You can always zoom in further. After thousandths (0.001) come ten-thousandths (0.0001), then hundred-thousandths (0.00001), then millionths (0.000001), and so on forever.

In real life, we usually only need a few decimal places, but mathematically, there is no limit.

⭐ Tier 3, Challenge
Q13
Comparing decimals
Which is larger: 0.45 or 0.405? Explain how you know.
0.45 is larger
✅

Compare tenths first: both have 4 tenths. Compare hundredths: 0.45 has 5 hundredths, but 0.405 has 0 hundredths. 5 hundredths > 0 hundredths, so 0.45 > 0.405.

Q14
Comparing decimals
Which is larger: 0.8 or 0.72?
0.8 is larger
✅

0.8 = 0.80 (8 tenths). 0.72 = 7 tenths and 2 hundredths. 8 tenths > 7 tenths, so 0.8 > 0.72. More digits doesn't mean larger!

Q15
Place value reasoning
The digit 5 appears in both 0.5 and 0.05. How many times larger is the 5 in 0.5 than the 5 in 0.05?
10 times larger
✅

In 0.5, the 5 is in the tenths place (value = 0.5). In 0.05, the 5 is in the hundredths place (value = 0.05). Since 0.5 ÷ 0.05 = 10, the 5 in 0.5 is 10 times larger.
