Learning Intention & Success Criteria

Learning Intention

We are learning to use levelling (compensation) to transform addition equations into simpler ones using friendly numbers.

Success Criteria

  • I know that levelling and compensation mean the same thing
  • I can take an amount from one number and give it to another to make a friendly number
  • I can use this strategy to solve addition and subtraction problems faster

The Big Idea

We can move amounts between numbers to create friendly numbers. The total stays the same, but the calculation gets easier!

This is called levelling, or compensation.

Card 5 of 18
Levelling

What it means

Taking an amount from one number and giving it to another to make both numbers easier to work with.

Example: 48 + 35

48 + 35
↓ Move 2 from 35 to 48 ↓
50 + 33 = 83
Card 6 of 18
Compensation

What it means

Another name for levelling. We compensate by moving an amount from one number to the other, keeping the total the same.

Example: 497 + 283

497 + 283
↓ Move 3 from 283 to 497 ↓
500 + 280 = 780
Card 7 of 18
Friendly Numbers

What they are

Numbers that are easy to work with in our heads. They are usually multiples of 10, 100, or 1,000.

10 20 100 500 1,000

Goal: Transform at least one number into a friendly number.

Card 8 of 18

The Golden Rule

What you must do
Add to one,
subtract the same from the other

For subtraction, we follow the same rule: constant difference strategy works the same way.

Card 9 of 18

How to spot when to use levelling

Look for numbers that are close to friendly numbers (nearly friendly!).

197 398 5,983 4.95

These are all nearly friendly numbers. They are just a few away from 200, 400, 6,000, and 5.00.

1

Spot the nearly friendly number: 399 is only 1 away from 400.

2

Take 1 from 246 and give it to 399: 400 + 245

3

Now it is easy! 400 + 245 = 645
