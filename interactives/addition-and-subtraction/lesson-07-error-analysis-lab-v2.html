4

Step 2 and 3: Compare and Diagnose

The Check

Radar says: ≈ 4,600 Student's answer: 4,785
The answer is close to the radar, but we should check the working carefully.

Diagnosis

There are two zeros in the tens and hundreds places. Trading is tricky here. Ones: 4 - 9 needs a trade. Tens place is 0, so we must trade from the hundreds. But hundreds is also 0, so we must trade from the thousands. After trading: thousands becomes 6, hundreds becomes 9, tens becomes 10. Then trade 1 ten to the ones. Ones becomes 14. Now: 14 - 9 = 5, (10-1) - 8 = 1 (not 8 as written), 9 - 3 = 6, 6 - 2 = 4. The student made an error in the tens place and didn't properly manage the cascade of trades.

The answer 4,653 is too large. This is a difficult subtraction with zeros. Ones: 0 − 7 needs a trade. But tens is 0, hundreds is 2. Wrap Up: The Big Question

Think About It

Why is estimation (our Radar) so powerful for finding errors? Could we spot every mistake just by looking at the algorithm alone?

The Key Message

Estimation is a safety check for every calculation. If your answer doesn't match your radar estimate, something has gone wrong. Then we trace column by column to find exactly where and why.
