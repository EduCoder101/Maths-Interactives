Learning Intention & Success Criteria

Learning Intention

We are learning to apply our chosen strategies independently whilst identifying extraneous (useless) information in word problems.

Success Criteria

  • I can ignore numbers in a word problem that do not help me solve it
  • I can work independently to solve problems using the most efficient strategy
  • I can justify why I chose a specific strategy for a specific problem

The Messy Problem

Solve this. All the information you need is here.

John is 12 years old. He has 3 dogs. He goes to the shop with $20.00 and buys a toy for $14.50. It takes him 15 minutes to walk home.
How much change does he have?

The Term

Numbers in a word problem that do not help you solve it are called Extraneous Information. Real-world problems and test questions often include them. Your job is to identify them and cross them out before you calculate.

What is a Problem Solving Clinic?

Today the class runs as three simultaneous streams. You will work on the problem set that is right for where you are. The teacher will run short, focused workshops at the front for students who need extra support.

Tier 1
Whole Number Mastery
2-step word problems using whole numbers, populations, shopping scenarios, distances. Focus: clean algorithm work with trading across zeros.
Tier 2
Proficient Decimals
Bank account-style problems using decimals to 2 places. Deposits, withdrawals, and running balances. Focus: decimal point alignment in the written algorithm.
Tier 3
Extension
Missing Digit puzzles and 3-step decimal problems. Use inverse operations to reason backwards and find unknown digits in a completed algorithm.

The Non-Negotiable Rule

For Every Single Question

Regardless of which tier you are on, the Radar for Error (Estimation) must be written down before you calculate. This is not optional. Every question. Every time.

Tier 1, Whole Number Mastery
2-Step Whole Number Problems
Tier 1 · Problem A
A school has 1,205 students. At the start of Term 3, 48 students enrol and 23 students leave to move to another school. The school has 14 classrooms. How many students are at the school at the end of enrolment?
Note: Extraneous information is hidden in this problem.
Extraneous: 14 classrooms (not needed). Useful: 1,205 students + 48 enrolled - 23 left. Estimate: 1,200 + 50 - 20 = 1,230.
1,230 students.
1,205 + 48 = 1,253
1,253 - 23 = 1,230
Tier 1, Whole Number Mastery
2-Step Whole Number Problems
Tier 1 · Problem B
A fruit shop starts the week with 2,400 kg of produce. On Monday they sell 347 kg. On Tuesday they receive a delivery of 580 kg. The shop has been open for 12 years. How much produce is in the shop after Tuesday's delivery?
Extraneous: 12 years. Two steps: subtract sales, then add the delivery. Estimate first!
2,633 kg.
2,400 - 347 = 2,053
2,053 + 580 = 2,633
Tier 1, Whole Number Mastery
2-Step Whole Number Problems
Tier 1 · Problem C
A council has a budget of $45,000 for road repairs. They spend $18,760 fixing potholes and $9,400 repainting lines. Road works take 6 days to complete. How much budget remains?
Extraneous: 6 days. Add both expenses first, then subtract from $45,000. Or subtract twice in sequence, same result!
$16,840 remaining.
18,760 + 9,400 = 28,160
45,000 - 28,160 = 16,840
Tier 2, Proficient Decimals
Bank Account Problems
Tier 2 · Problem A
Maya's bank account has a balance of $450.75. She deposits $120.00. Then she withdraws $35.50 to pay for a birthday present. What is her final balance?
Two steps: add the deposit, then subtract the withdrawal. Line up the decimal points. Estimate: $450 + $120 - $35 = $535.
$535.25
$450.75 + $120.00 = $570.75
$570.75 - $35.50 = $535.25
Tier 2, Proficient Decimals
Bank Account Problems
Tier 2 · Problem B
Tom starts with $1,250.00 in his account. He deposits his pay of $487.50. He then pays three bills: electricity $142.80, internet $59.95, and phone $45.00. What is his balance after paying all three bills?
Add the pay first. Then add all three bills together (hidden step!) and subtract the total. Or subtract each bill in sequence.
$1,489.75
$1,250.00 + $487.50 = $1,737.50
$142.80 + $59.95 + $45.00 = $247.75
$1,737.50 - $247.75 = $1,489.75
Tier 2, Proficient Decimals
Bank Account Problems
Tier 2 · Problem C
A school fundraising account has $3,045.60. The school runs two stalls and earns $284.50 and $196.75. They spend $512.40 on new library books. How much is left in the account?
Add both stall earnings to the starting amount, then subtract the book purchase. Three decimal numbers to add, line up those points!
$3,014.45
$284.50 + $196.75 = $481.25
$3,045.60 + $481.25 = $3,526.85
$3,526.85 - $512.40 = $3,014.45

Tier 3, Extension
3-Step Decimal Problems
Tier 3 · Problem C
A landscaper has a budget of $2,500.00. She buys 3 bags of soil at $24.75 each, a garden hose for $38.90, and 4 paving stones at $15.60 each. How much of her budget remains?
Two hidden steps: 3 x $24.75 and 4 x $15.60. Find all individual totals first, add them together, then subtract from $2,500.
$2,324.45 remaining.
3 x $24.75 = $74.25
4 x $15.60 = $62.40
$74.25 + $38.90 + $62.40 = $175.55
$2,500.00 - $175.55 = $2,324.45

The Key Point

There is no single correct tool. The question is about efficiency and accuracy. A number like $450.75 + $120.00 can be done mentally. Three numbers with different decimal places might warrant a written algorithm. Many separate transactions are exactly what a calculator is designed for. A mathematician chooses. They do not just default to one method every time.

Vocabulary Recap

New term today
Extraneous Information
Numbers or details in a word problem that are not needed to find the answer. Their purpose is to distract. Your job is to ignore them.
From Lesson 8
Hidden Step
A calculation buried inside the problem that must be completed before you can find the final answer. Often signalled by words like "each", "both", "same way".
