Learning Intention and Success Criteria

Learning Intention

We are learning to use the area model to partition and multiply 2 and 3 digit numbers by a one digit number.

Success Criteria

  • I can draw and label an area model to represent a multiplication.
  • I can partition a number into hundreds, tens, and ones and multiply each part separately.
  • I can add the partial products to find the final answer.
  • I can explain how the area model connects to the formal algorithm.

The Tiled Floor Problem

The school hall is 24 metres long and 6 metres wide. How many square metres of tiles are needed?

Most of you would try 24 times 6 straight away. But what if we could see what this looks like as a shape?

24
20
20 times 6= 120
4
4 times 6= 24
6
Total: 120 + 24 = 144 square metres
Card 2 of 9

The Distributive Property

What we just did has a formal name: the distributive property.

We distributed the multiplication across a sum:

6 times 24 = 6 times (20 + 4)
= 120 + 24
= 144

Instead of one hard multiplication, we get two easy ones.

Key vocabulary

Each smaller product (120 and 24) is called a partial product. We add all the partial products to get the final product.

The formal algorithm

Here is 47 times 8 solved with the formal written algorithm:

Written algorithm 4 7 times 8 3 7 6

The answer is 376. But why does this work? What is actually happening at each step?

Estimate first

50 times 8 = 400

Rounded up from 47, so the real answer is a little less than 400. Our algorithm answer of 376 is close. Radar confirmed.

Think about it
Discussion

How could you work out 47 times 8 without using the formal algorithm? What if you broke 47 apart into friendlier numbers?

47 is made up of 40 and 7. What if we multiplied each part by 8 separately?

The area model

Let's build the area model for 47 times 8, one piece at a time.

47
40
40 times 8= 320
7
7 times 8= 56
8
Total: 320 + 56 = 376
5
Connecting the two
The connection

The algorithm is a compressed version of the area model. In the algorithm, when you multiply 8 times 7 = 56, that is the right hand section of the area model. When you multiply 8 times 40 = 320, that is the left hand section. The algorithm just writes it more compactly.

Algorithm 4 7 times 8 3 7 6
8 times 7 = 56 → right box
8 times 40 = 320 → left box

The connection
Algorithm = compressed area model

In the algorithm, multiplying the ones digit (4 times 6 = 24) gives the right box. Multiplying the tens (4 times 30 = 120) gives the middle box. Multiplying the hundreds (4 times 200 = 800) gives the left box. Same maths, different layout.

Estimation check

We estimated 960. Our answer is 944. The difference is only 16. Radar confirmed.

Go Deeper

You are ready to think like a mathematician. These tasks will help you spot errors, test conjecture, and justify your reasoning.

Task 1: Specialising

Your Challenge
A student split 47 times 8 into (40 times 8) + (7 times 8) and got:
40 times 8 = 360
7 times 8 = 56
Total = 416
Is the area model correct? Find and explain any error.
Model Answer

The error is in the first partial product. 40 times 8 = 320, not 360. You might have multiplied 45 times 8 by mistake, or added 40 + 8 instead of multiplying them.

The correct calculation is:

40 times 8 = 320
7 times 8 = 56
Total = 320 + 56 = 376
Mathematician's Tip: Always double check each partial product. A small multiplication error gets carried through to the final answer!

Task 2: Conjecturing

Your Challenge
"Splitting a number into more parts in the area model always gives a different final answer."
Is this statement Always true, Sometimes true, or Never true? Use 47 times 8 to show your thinking.
Model Answer

Never true. The total is always the same regardless of how you partition the numbers. Here's why:

Let me try 47 times 8 three different ways:

(40 + 7) times 8 = (40 times 8) + (7 times 8) = 320 + 56 = 376

(30 + 17) times 8 = (30 times 8) + (17 times 8) = 240 + 136 = 376

(20 + 20 + 7) times 8 = (20 times 8) + (20 times 8) + (7 times 8) = 160 + 160 + 56 = 376

Every way gives 376! The partition doesn't matter. Only the original numbers (47 and 8) determine the answer.

Big Idea: This is because multiplication is distributive. 47 times 8 equals the same thing no matter how you split 47.

Task 3: Justifying

Your Challenge
A student says that 99 times 7 is easier to solve with the area model by splitting it as (100 - 1) times 7 instead of (90 + 9) times 7.
Do you agree? Justify your reasoning by showing the calculation both ways.
Model Answer

I agree that the first method is easier. Both ways give the same answer, but (100 - 1) times 7 is faster for mental maths.

Method 1: (100 - 1) times 7

(100 - 1) times 7 = (100 times 7) - (1 times 7) = 700 - 7 = 693

Method 2: (90 + 9) times 7

(90 + 9) times 7 = (90 times 7) + (9 times 7) = 630 + 63 = 693

Both equal 693, but Method 1 is faster because 100 times 7 is easier to calculate mentally than 90 times 7. For numbers close to 100 or 1000, this trick is very handy!

Mathematician's Tip: Look for numbers that are close to a "friendly" number like 10, 100, or 1000. Splitting using complementary subtraction can be faster than addition.
