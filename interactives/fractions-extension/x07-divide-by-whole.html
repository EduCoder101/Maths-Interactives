Year 5 Fractions Extension

Dividing a Fraction by a Whole Number

Share a fractional part into equal pieces
Learning Intention and Success Criteria ▼

Learning Intention

We are learning to divide a fraction by a whole number by sharing the fractional part into equal pieces.

Success Criteria

  • I can see dividing by a whole number as sharing a part into equal pieces.
  • I can keep the numerator and multiply the denominator by the whole number.
  • I can simplify the answer.
  • I can solve worded sharing problems.
Learn this skill ▼

Dividing a fraction by a whole number means sharing that part into equal pieces. To share 34 between 2 people, cut every piece into smaller pieces so it shares evenly. The shortcut is to keep the numerator and multiply the denominator by the whole number. The answer is always smaller, because each piece is being split up.

Worked example: 34 ÷ 2
1Cut each piece so it shares evenly.
Cut every quarter in half to make eighths. Now 34 is 68.
2Share into equal groups.
Share the 6 eighths between 2: each share is 3 eighths.
3Read off the answer.
34 ÷ 2 = 38. Rule: keep the 3, and 4 × 2 = 8.
Why the denominator grows

Sharing into 2 cuts every piece into 2, so there are twice as many pieces and each is half the size. That is why 34 ÷ 2 becomes 38: the denominator doubles.

Go Deeper: Dividing Investigations

These tasks push your thinking beyond getting the answer. Explore, test claims, and reason like a mathematician.

Specialising
Work out 1/2 ÷ 2, 1/2 ÷ 3, and 1/2 ÷ 4.
What happens to the answer as you share between more people? Why?
A mathematician's response

1/2 ÷ 2 = 1/4, 1/2 ÷ 3 = 1/6, 1/2 ÷ 4 = 1/8.

The more people I share between, the smaller each share. That makes sense: the same half is being cut into more pieces, so each piece is tinier. I also noticed 1/2 ÷ n = 1/(2n).

Mathematician's thinking: You are specialising. You used ordered cases to see how sharing changes the result.
Justifying
A student says 6/8 ÷ 2 = 3/4, because they divided the top and the bottom by 2.
Is the student right? Explain what dividing the top and bottom by 2 actually does.
A mathematician's response

Dividing the top and bottom by 2 turns 6/8 into 3/4, but those are equal fractions. The amount has not changed at all, so that cannot be the answer to dividing by 2.

To actually share 6/8 between 2, I keep the numerator and double the denominator: 6/16 = 3/8. A good check: half of 6/8 should be less than 6/8, and 3/8 is, but 3/4 is not.

Mathematician's thinking: You are justifying. You separated simplifying (same value) from dividing (smaller value).
Generalising
Write a rule for a/b ÷ n, and explain why the denominator is multiplied.
Use the idea of cutting each piece into more pieces.
A mathematician's response

My rule: a/b ÷ n = a/(b × n). Keep the numerator, multiply the denominator by n.

It works because sharing into n equal groups cuts every 1/b piece into n smaller pieces. Each new piece is 1/(b × n), and I still have a of the original pieces worth, so the share is a/(b × n).

Mathematician's thinking: You are generalising. You wrote a rule with letters and tied it to the picture.
